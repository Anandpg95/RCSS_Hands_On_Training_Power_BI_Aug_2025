Course Description

Microsoft Power BI is quickly becoming one of the world's most powerful self-service business intelligence platforms, as well as an absolutely essential tool for today's top data professionals.

This course is your hands-on, project-based guide to mastering Power BI Desktop. We'll follow a clear, systematic progression through the entire business intelligence workflow, and break down our process into four key stages:

  1. Connect and transform the raw source data

  2. Design and build a relational model

  3. Analyze the data with calculated columns & DAX measures

  4. Design custom, interactive reports and dashboards

Whether you're a casual Excel user, aspiring analyst, or data professional, this is the only course you need to build job-ready Power BI skills.

WHO SHOULD TAKE THIS COURSE?

  • Excel users who want to develop advanced analytics and data modeling skills, or transition into a business intelligence role

  • Analysts or BI professionals looking for a self-service platform to analyze large datasets, connect to external sources, build relational models, or design custom dashboards

  • Anyone seeking a clear and straight-forward introduction to Power BI Desktop

WHAT ARE THE COURSE REQUIREMENTS?

  • Power BI Desktop for PC/Windows (Power BI is currently unavailable for Mac)

  • Experience with Power Query, Power Pivot & DAX is a plus, but not required

  • No advance preparation is required (we'll walk through set-up and install)

WHAT ARE THE COURSE OBJECTIVES?

  • Identify basic Power BI functionality, including data sources, contextual menus, regional settings, and differences between Power BI Desktop & Service

  • Identify common Power Query tools and use cases for adding and transforming columns, including grouping, merging, appending, and transforming text and dates

  • Identify common data modeling tools and best practices, including table names, relationships, hierarchies, data categories, cardinality, and filter flow

  • Identify examples of filter context and various filtering methods, including user roles, parameters, and drillthrough filters

  • Identify DAX syntax, when to use common functions, performance implications, and differences between measures and calculated columns

  • Identify and interpret key Power BI visuals, including configuration options, interaction modes, and common use cases

  • Identify common optimization techniques, including pausing visuals, applying presets, analyzing performance, and leveraging external tools
